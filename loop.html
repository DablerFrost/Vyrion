Module 07 · E-Waste
Agbogbloshie, Ghana
15,000 workers
One of the world's largest informal e-waste processing sites. 215,000 tonnes of e-waste processed annually using open burning. Lead concentrations in soil 18× WHO safety limit. 80% of global e-waste shipped to Global South for informal processing. Basel Convention violations documented.
Source: Global E-Waste Monitor 2024 · Blacksmith Institute · Pure Earth
Module 07 · Soil Contamination
PFAS Contamination · Global
45 countries
Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances ("forever chemicals") detected in soil and groundwater across 45 countries. 200+ active compounds. EPA classified PFOA and PFOS as hazardous in 2024. US cleanup liability estimated at $400B. EU PFAS restriction enters force 2026.
Source: EPA PFAS Program · EEA PFAS Action Plan · Science Advances 2023
Module 07 · Ocean Plastic
Single-Use Plastic Crisis
36 % of plastic
36% of all plastic produced is single-use packaging. 85% ends as waste within one year. Only 14% collected for recycling globally. UN Global Plastics Treaty negotiations ongoing — 175 nations engaged. OECD forecasts plastic waste doubling by 2060 without systemic change.
Source: OECD Global Plastics Outlook 2022 · UNEP Plastics Treaty 2024
Module 07 · Best Practice
Germany Circular Model
66 % recycled
Germany recycles 66% of its municipal waste — highest rate globally. Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) legislation mandates manufacturer take-back for electronics, batteries, and packaging. Green Dot (Grüner Punkt) scheme collects 2.3 Mt packaging/yr. Circular economy contribution: €60B GDP.
Source: Eurostat Waste Statistics 2023 · German Federal Environment Agency
Module 07 · Landfill Gas
Methane from Landfills
~11% of CH₄
Landfills are the 3rd largest source of anthropogenic methane globally, contributing ~11% of total CH₄ emissions. US landfills emit 109 Mt CO₂-equivalent annually. Only 35% of landfill gas captured globally. Landfill gas-to-energy projects operational at 1,000+ US sites.
Source: EPA LMOP Program · IPCC AR6 · IEA Methane Tracker 2024
Module 07 · Food Waste
Food Loss & Waste Crisis
1.3 Bt / yr
1.3 billion tonnes of food lost or wasted annually — enough to feed 3 billion people. Food waste generates 4.4 Gt CO₂-equivalent/yr (8% of global GHG). In HIC, consumer waste dominates. In LIC, supply-chain loss dominates. UNEP FWI 2024 confirms no measurable global reduction since 2011.
Source: UNEP Food Waste Index 2024 · FAO Food Loss Database

Methodology
"Architecture without promises."
Every LOOP indicator is source-cited, timestamped, and published before the event. All waste metrics draw exclusively from UNITAR, EPA, Eurostat, World Bank, and OECD — all public, open-access datasets. Scores are computed against 2000 baseline levels. Every correction is published publicly.
