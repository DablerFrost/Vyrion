Deployment Protocol · Four Stages

How FLUX Deploys

Cities and industrial operators license FLUX systems through a four-stage protocol — from ATLAS-guided site assessment to verified ongoing VYRION reporting. Every deployment enters the Open Proof Protocol ledger on day one.

01Assessment
ATLAS-Guided Siting & System Design
FLUX draws on ATLAS real-time environmental data to conduct energy demand analysis, grid capacity review, and optimal system selection. Solar arrays sited for maximum irradiance. Electrolyser units sized to available renewable power. DAC units positioned for highest atmospheric density. All assessment data enters the Open Proof Protocol ledger from day one.
8–12 weeks
02Licensing
Capacity License Agreement & Permitting
Operators execute a FLUX capacity license — fixed annual fee per MW solar, per MW electrolyser, or per kt CO₂/yr capture licensed. Output variability is FLUX's risk, not the operator's. FLUX handles all regulatory permitting frameworks, grid connection agreements, and environmental documentation. Predictable infrastructure cost from signature date.
4–8 weeks
03Installation
Modular Deployment & Commissioning
Prefabricated FLUX units shipped and installed by certified deployment teams. Each unit arrives pre-tested and pre-configured. Grid integration, storage connection, and monitoring hardware installed in parallel. No custom engineering required on-site. Commissioning verification logged immediately into VYRION ATLAS telemetry.
6–24 weeks
04Verification
Live VYRION Monitoring & Reporting
All generation, capture, and emissions displacement data continuously telemetered into the VYRION platform. Operators receive verified carbon accounting reports, CONSCIENCE score integration, and annual third-party audit packages. Find an error — we publish the correction within 24 hours.
Continuous
