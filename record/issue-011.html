Pledge vs. Reality
PLEDGE Unilever has committed to net zero emissions across its full value chain by 2039 — eleven years ahead of the Paris Agreement's 2050 benchmark. The company has pledged zero deforestation across its supply chain and a 50% reduction in absolute operational emissions by 2030 from a 2015 baseline. These targets are science-aligned and formally approved by the Science Based Targets initiative at the 1.5°C pathway.
Unilever Climate Transition Action Plan 2023 · SBTi Approved Targets · Annual Report 2024
REALITY Unilever has achieved a verified 23% reduction in absolute operational emissions since 2015, placing it materially ahead of many consumer goods peers. Scope 3 emissions — which represent the majority of Unilever's footprint across agriculture, ingredients, and consumer use — are improving at a measured but slower pace. Palm oil supply chain traceability remains an active concern, with residual deforestation risk exposure acknowledged in third-party assessments. One active climate-related lawsuit is recorded in the Sabin Center database.
Unilever Annual Report 2024 · CDP 2023 · Supply Chain Due Diligence Disclosures · Sabin Center Database

Key Findings
F — 01 · Pledge Architecture
Unilever holds one of the strongest pledge architectures in consumer goods
A 2039 net zero commitment covering the full value chain, SBTi-approved at 1.5°C, with formal interim targets through 2030. This is a structurally credible, independently validated climate pathway — distinguishing Unilever from peers whose targets remain unvalidated or limited to operational scope alone.
Strength Signal
F — 02 · Emissions Progress
Operational progress is real, absolute, and verified
The 23% reduction in absolute operational emissions since 2015 reflects genuine infrastructure investment across energy efficiency, renewable procurement, and manufacturing decarbonization. This is not a relative intensity metric — it is verified absolute reduction at global operating scale.
Verified Progress
F — 03 · Structural Risk
Scope 3 remains the execution pressure point
Agricultural supply chain inputs, consumer product use-phase, and disposal dominate Unilever's emissions footprint — categories where direct corporate control is inherently limited. Palm oil traceability continues generating third-party risk flags. Scope 3 improvement must materially accelerate to honor the 2039 pathway.
Watch Closely

Capital Markets Signal

Unilever sits inside the ON TRACK band at 72/100 — the highest score published in The Record series to date. The score reflects a company that has built credible climate infrastructure: a science-validated target, near-leadership disclosure quality, constructive policy engagement, and measurable operational progress.

The primary execution variable is Scope 3. Agricultural commodity sourcing, palm oil supply chains, and consumer use-phase emissions represent the majority of Unilever's climate footprint and the zone of greatest execution risk. Pledge architecture is strong; downstream delivery is the test.

At current trajectory, Unilever's operational emissions pathway is credible. Scope 3 convergence before 2039 is feasible but not yet verified — it depends on supplier engagement at scale, land-use transition, and ingredient reformulation across a portfolio of hundreds of consumer brands.

Investable Signal: High pledge quality. Near-leadership disclosure. Constructive lobbying posture. Low legal exposure. Primary risk: Scope 3 supply chain execution and palm oil deforestation residuals. Watch for: updated Scope 3 trajectories, CDP 2024 response, SBTi interim milestone progress, and deforestation-free supply chain verification reports.
