Key Findings

Finding 01 — Renewable matching vs. absolute demand. 100% renewable electricity matching does not eliminate grid-level emissions impact when total consumption rises significantly. Absolute demand growth is now the dominant variable. Meta's procurement model was structured in a pre-AI scaling environment — that environment no longer exists.

Finding 02 — AI is the new emissions driver. AI infrastructure expansion is increasing energy intensity across the technology sector. Meta is among the most exposed due to scale of deployment. Training and inference workloads are driving sustained increases in electricity demand that outpace reported efficiency gains.

Finding 03 — Scope 3 trajectory is reversing. Supply chain and energy system dependencies are pushing Scope 3 emissions upward, conflicting directly with net zero pathway assumptions. Absolute emissions are rising while the 2030 pledge requires absolute reductions. This is the structural divergence CONSCIENCE is designed to surface.

Finding 04 — The pledge was built for a different era. Meta's 2030 net zero commitment did not anticipate AI-scale compute growth. The constraint is no longer procurement — it is total system load. Gas-linked power agreements signed in 2024 represent a structural regression that partially offsets the procurement gains achieved since 2020.

Capital Markets Signal

Meta sits in the WARNING band — a structural divergence signal, not a terminal failure. The pledge quality is genuine: 2030 net zero is one of the more aggressive timelines in the technology sector, and 100% renewable matching since 2020 reflects early, real commitments. The problem is the energy profile has materially changed since those commitments were made.

The investable risk: renewable procurement continues, but absolute demand — and associated grid-level and Scope 3 emissions — are rising. Under tightening 1.5°C regulatory frameworks, absolute emissions count — not matching percentages. Gas-linked power agreements signed in 2024 are the clearest signal of structural regression visible in current filings.

Investable Signal: Elevated transition risk in high-growth, AI-exposed tech. Opportunity cost versus higher-scoring ON TRACK regenerators executing faster absolute decarbonization. Watch for: Scope 3 inflection in next sustainability filing. Verifiable clean-energy data center agreements replacing gas-linked supply. AI efficiency gains (tokens per watt) that demonstrably slow absolute demand growth.
