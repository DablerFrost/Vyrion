Verification Architecture
Satellite-first · No self-reporting · ATLAS native
Every PULSE credit is verified against ATLAS satellite data before issuance. The voluntary carbon market's systemic failure was delegating verification to auditors with inherent conflicts of interest. PULSE removes the auditor. ESA Sentinel-2, TROPOMI, and Landsat data are the auditors — open, reproducible, and non-negotiable.
Tokenization Architecture
Open standard · Non-custodial · VYRION Ledger
PULSE credits are issued as open-standard tokens on the VYRION Public Ledger — not locked to any proprietary blockchain. The ledger is designed for institutional interoperability: PULSE credits can be held in compliance registries, custodied by standard financial institutions, or presented directly as evidence of retirement in CONSCIENCE reports.
Market Integrity Layer
Double-counting prevention · Additionality proof · Permanence monitoring
PULSE's market integrity layer addresses all three structural failures of the voluntary carbon market simultaneously: double-counting (impossible — each credit has a unique satellite-verified hash), additionality (ATLAS dynamic baselines, not static estimates), and permanence (continuous ATLAS monitoring; credits auto-decay if reversal is detected).
CONSCIENCE Demand Layer
Real-time retirement → corporate score signal
Corporate credit retirement in PULSE generates an immediate CONSCIENCE score update — removing the annual reporting lag that makes current corporate ESG claims unverifiable in real time. A company that retires 10,000 tCO₂e in PULSE sees its CONSCIENCE score update the same day. Accountability and action on the same clock.
